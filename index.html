System Overview

The Smart BSF Rearing Cage is an automated, multi-stage environmental control system designed for the efficient cultivation of Black Soldier Fly (Hermetia illucens). The system is divided into three distinct compartments — Adult, Pupa, and Larvae & Egg — each with dedicated sensors and actuators calibrated to the specific needs of each life stage.


An Arduino Uno handles real-time sensor acquisition and relay control (actuators). A Raspberry Pi 5 serves as the central hub, aggregating data and hosting the web interface for remote monitoring and control. A solar power system with a 12V 25Ah battery provides energy independence.

Solar Power System

☀️ Solar Panel

100W monocrystalline panel, positioned for maximum photovoltaic intake. Primary energy source for daytime operations.

⚡ Battery Bank

12V 25Ah Lithium-ion battery pack. Provides 10–12 hours of autonomous operation as backup when solar input is insufficient.

🔧 Charge Controller

Solar charge controller configured to manage battery charging and prevent over-discharge for the Li-ion pack.

⚠️ Battery Runtime Note The 12V 25Ah battery provides an estimated 10–12 hours of full-system backup. Monitor battery voltage through the web dashboard, especially during extended cloudy periods. Do not fully discharge the battery below its minimum voltage cutoff.

🔌 DC Breaker Panel

The system has 3 DC breakers. Each controls a different segment of the power distribution. They must be switched on in a specific sequence to prevent inrush damage and ensure proper initialization.

🚨 CRITICAL — Follow Breaker Sequence Exactly Switching breakers in the wrong order may cause damage to the controller, sensors, or actuators. Always follow the startup procedure in the next section.
▶️

System Startup Procedure

⚠️ Before You Begin Ensure all wiring connections are secure and no exposed conductors are present. Verify the battery voltage is adequate (≥ 11.5V) before powering on.
01

BREAKER 2 — Middle Breaker (First)

Switch ON the middle (2nd) breaker first. This activates the Solar Charge Controller and battery management circuit. Wait for the charge controller display to initialize before proceeding.

02

BREAKER 1 — First Breaker (Second)

Switch ON the first (1st) breaker. This connects the main power distribution to the system loads. Confirm no fault indicators appear.

03

BREAKER 3 — Last Breaker (Third)

Switch ON the last (3rd) breaker. This powers the Arduino Uno, Raspberry Pi 5, and all sensors and relay modules. Allow the system ~30–60 seconds to fully boot.

04

Verify System Boot

Confirm the Raspberry Pi 5 has booted (status LED steady). Open the web dashboard from a connected device to verify all sensor readings are live and actuators are responsive.

Shutdown Sequence To power down safely, reverse the startup order: turn OFF Breaker 3 first → then Breaker 1 → then Breaker 2 last.

Safety & Maintenance Notes

🚨 Breaker Sequence Must Be Followed Never bypass or skip the 3-step breaker startup sequence. Always: Breaker 2 → Breaker 1 → Breaker 3. Reverse the order to power down.
🚨 Active LOW Relay Logic These relays activate on LOW signal. Never send continuous HIGH signals expecting actuators to be "safe off" without verifying relay state through the dashboard.
⚠️ Battery Voltage Monitoring Check battery voltage regularly. Do not allow it to drop below 11.0V. Extended discharge can permanently damage Lithium-ion cells.
⚠️ Humidity Near Electronics Humidifiers are active inside the cage. Ensure all sensor modules and relay boards are positioned away from direct mist exposure. Use conformal coating if available.
✅ Regular Sensor Calibration DHT22 sensors should be cross-checked against a reference thermometer/hygrometer monthly. Replace sensors showing consistent drift of more than ±2°C or ±5% RH.
✅ Solar Panel Maintenance Clean the solar panel surface monthly to remove dust and debris that reduce photovoltaic efficiency. Inspect wiring for corrosion quarterly.
